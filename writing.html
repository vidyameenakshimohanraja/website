The heart and the bottle: A beautiful lesson about loss and grief

The Heart and the Bottle is an exquisitely thoughtful and heartwarming tale about the endearing bond between a young girl and her grandfather. The story unfolds with the protagonist embarking on enchanting escapades alongside her grandad, venturing into lush forests, stargazing in moonlit parks, and enjoying carefree swims in the ocean.

The illustration in a picture book holds immense significance, just like the story itself. As noted by children's literature scholars Maria Nikolajeva and Carole Scott, the distinctive nature of picture books as an art form is rooted in the fusion of visual and verbal elements. Oliver Jeffers truly excels in harmonizing these aspects, crafting captivating narratives. Through intricate montages of imagination portrayed in delicate circles, readers can appreciate the illustrator's heartfelt commitment to depicting the girl's innocence and the wonders of imagination. The gentle pastel hues and airy, tender sketches make it an ideal choice for a comforting and serene reading experience.

Apart from the stunning visuals that immediately captivate the audience, the story itself is meticulously crafted in a way that resonates with both the young and the old. It's expressed with such artistry, making everything literal yet intricately beautiful. The protagonist, after the loss of her grandfather, carries her heart in a bottle hung around her neck, symbolizing the act of "bottling up feelings." This serves as a powerful emblem for children grappling with emotions and loss.

Death is an early experience for many children, often through the loss of a pet or grandparent. While some may feel it's too complex of a concept for 5–6 year olds, this book's hopeful narrative enables children to understand death, accept it, and embrace change as something natural, inevitable, and ultimately beautiful.

Rather than dwelling on absence, the story delicately illustrates the importance of filling the voids in our life with boundless love and cherished memories, creating a space filled with warmth and nostalgia. All in all, it's truly a heartwarming book with imaginative illustrations and a valuable lesson that resonates with both children and adults for a lifetime.

Lady Whistledown in the spotlight: What is happening in Bridgerton season 3
Hannah Dodd as Francesca Bridgerton and Nicola Coughlan as Penelope Featherington — Laurence Cendrowicz/Netflix

Hold onto your bonnets, dearest gentle readers—Bridgerton Season 3 is back, and it's more dazzling than ever. Premiering on May 16th, this season unfurls new feathers and blooms with romance that's sure to keep us all talking.

This time, it's Penelope Featherington, portrayed by the ever-charming Nicola Coughlan, who takes center stage. Unlike Daphne (Phoebe Dynevor), Kate Sharma (Simone Ashley), or this season's Francesca Bridgerton, Penelope's third year in the marriage market is notably devoid of suitors. But this wallflower's sights are set firmly on her childhood friend, Colin Bridgerton.

Colin, fresh from his globetrotting adventures, returns to London to a flurry of female admirers. Meanwhile, Penelope remains in the shadows, observing the societal frenzy from her usual corner.

Over at the Featherington household, things are in dire straits, with the family desperate for a male heir to keep the estate afloat. But Penelope has other plans — determined to find a husband and leave her family home. Thus, in regencified and Princess Diaries fashion, Penelope ditches her infamous bright yellow gowns for colors that intriguingly match the Bridgerton palette.

The drama peaks at the season's first ball when mean girl Cressida Cowper publicly humiliates Penelope. Taking refuge in her alter ego, Lady Whistledown, she vents her frustrations in the scandal sheet, leaving Colin and best friend Eloise in shock. Colin, in a sincere bid to make amends, offers to help Penelope secure a husband.

In the season's final two episodes, Penelope catches the eye of the wealthy newcomer, Lord Debling (Sam Phillips). Despite winning his affection, Lord Debling senses her heart lies elsewhere. In a heartfelt carriage exchange, Colin apologizes and confesses his true feelings, culminating in a romantic proposal.

Everything You Need to Know About the Hit Drama Lovely Runner
Kim Hye Yoon as Im Sol and Byeon Woo Seok as Ryu SunJae — credits: tvN

Lovely Runner (선재 업고 튀어) is the latest sensation sweeping South Korean television. Directed by the acclaimed Yoon Jong-ho, known for Guardian: The Lonely and Great God and Flower of Evil, it debuted on tvN on April 8, 2024. The series follows Im Sol, who travels between the past and present to alter the fate of her beloved Ryu Sun-Jae.

Drawing inspiration from classics like A Time Called You and Twinkling Watermelon, the series is a delightful trip down memory lane. Set in the 2000s with nostalgia-inducing elements, it is the genuine heartfelt love story between Im-Sol and Sun-Jae that truly steals the show.

Plot Overview

The series kicks off with Sun-Jae, a member of the popular boy band Eclipse, giving a radio interview. He calls Im-Sol at the hospital recovering from a tragic accident, and gently encourages her to hold onto hope. This seemingly insignificant call sets off a chain reaction that forever alters their lives.

Fast forward a few years — Im-Sol has become a dedicated fan of Sun-Jae, but her happiness is short-lived as Sun-Jae takes his own life. In a desperate attempt to save him, she drops a watch at the hospital that turns out to be a time-travel device, propelling her into the past. Armed with knowledge of future events, she strives to prevent his death.

How it all began

The drama traces its origins back to Kim Bbang's webtoon Tomorrow's Best, adapted by Lee Sieun, known for True Beauty. The overwhelming success of Lovely Runner has cemented Lee Sieun's status as a powerhouse in the K-drama industry.
